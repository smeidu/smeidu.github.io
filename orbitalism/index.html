ORBITALISM

UX Vision for Future Space Tourists

ORBITALISM is a vision of how a space tourist user experience could look and feel.

It was a research project at the Copenhagen Institute of Interaction Design in 2012.

Interaction Design

People-centered design for the next generation of human space flight, with a focus on space tourism.

Service Design

A service design concept for a space tourism offering. Download the PDF.

Contributors

Franz Viehböck, Former Astronaut, Soyuz TM-12 Mission to Mir (1991). Interview and user testing and co-creation of ideas for a space tourist user experience.

Dr. Werner Balogh, Programme Officer at UN Office for Outer Space Affairs. Advised on international space law and the current state of the space industry.

Réka Kovács, Co-Founder at Nanosatisfi. Advised on the current state of the space industry, microgravity experience, and space tourism. Co-creation of ideas for a space tourist user experience.

Anders Højmose, Interaction Designer at TraceAgency. Project advisor.

Matt Cottam, CEO at Tellart. Advised on service design and user-centric design for astronauts.

Stefan Eder, Motion and 3D Designer at Flugfeld53. 3D visualization.

Christian Lendl, Multimedia Engineer and Tourism Specialist. Interview about the tourism industry and co-creation of ideas for a space tourist offering.

Sandra Hauplik-Meusburger, Architect. Member of the Space Architecture Technical Committee (SATC) of the American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics. Author of the book Architecture for Astronauts. Interview about the interface between people, space, and objects onboard a space station.

Remco Timmermans, Space Exploration Advocate and SpaceUpEU Organizer. Feedback and support for the project.
